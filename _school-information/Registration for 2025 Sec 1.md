<p>Dear Parents/Guardians,</p>
<p>A warm welcome on your child’s/ward’s posting to Kent Ridge Secondary
School!</p>
<p>Please note that students do not need to report to their posted secondary
schools upon receiving their <u>S1 posting results</u>.</p>
<p>The school will be using Parents Gateway (PG) to communicate with you
useful information on how to prepare your child/ward for the first day
of school.</p>
<p>Your child’s/ward’s <strong>2025 Sec 1 Class allocation</strong> will be <strong>available on</strong> Parents
Gateway after <strong>12 noon on 19 December 2024 (Thursday)</strong>.</p>
<p>As there may be student movement due to transfer cases, we seek your understanding
that there may be changes in the Form Class allocation. All Form Class
allocation will only be finalised after the school term begins.</p>
<p>You will also be notified via Parents Gateway if your child/ ward is eligible
to offer core subjects under SBB at a more demanding level, or Higher Mother
Tongue Languages HCL/HML. Please check the SBB and MTL sections below for
more information.</p>
<p>Do ensure books for the correct subject level are purchased for your child/ward.
If unsure, you may purchase textbooks in the first week of school.</p>
<p>For further clarification, please <a href="https://www.kentridgesec.moe.edu.sg/school-information/contact-information" rel="noopener nofollow" target="_blank">contact us</a>.</p>

<p>&lt;label for="accordion6"&gt;National Digital Literacy Programme (NDLP)&lt;/label&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;div&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;</p>
<p>Through the National Digital Literacy Programme (NDLP), all secondary
school students will own a school-prescribed Personal Learning Device (PLD).
Secondary one students would be invited to purchase a school-prescribed
PLD. Singapore Citizen students can use their Edusave Account to pay for
the PLD.</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>The PLD will be used in tandem with the national e-learning platform –
the Singapore Student Learning Space (“SLS”) – as well as other educational
technology tools to&amp;nbsp;personalise&amp;nbsp;and enhance students’
learning. This will also enable students to acquire digital skills and
apply these skills as they navigate an increasingly&amp;nbsp;digitalised&amp;nbsp;world.</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>For more information on Kent Ridge Secondary’s NDLP, please refer to&amp;nbsp;&lt;a
href="<a href="https://www.kentridgesec.moe.edu.sg/school-information/national-digital-literacy-programme-ndlp/&quot;>www.kentridgesec.moe.edu.sg/school-information/national-digital-literacy-programme-ndlp/</a>" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank">https://www.kentridgesec.moe.edu.sg/school-information/national-digital-literacy-programme-ndlp/"&gt;www.kentridgesec.moe.edu.sg/school-information/national-digital-literacy-programme-ndlp/&lt;/a&gt;</a>
</p>

<p>&lt;label for="accordion7"&gt;Art/Music Elective Programme (AEP/MEP)&lt;/label&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;div&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;</p>
<p>Secondary 1 students who exhibit interest and passion for Art/Music can
apply for Art/Music Elective Programme (AEP/MEP) in the following AEP/MEP&amp;nbsp;Centres:</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>• AEP: National Junior College&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>• MEP: Anglo-Chinese School (Independent)&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>• MEP:&amp;nbsp;Dunman&amp;nbsp;High School&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>The online application is open from 2-15 January 2024.</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>Successful applicants will attend weekly lessons in the afternoon at the
AEP/MEP Centre and are expected to commit to the programme for 4 years.
Prior to application, interested students and their parents should work
closely with the students’ secondary schools to ensure that their school
timetable and commitments would allow them to participate fully in the
AEP/MEP.</p>

<p>&lt;div&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;p&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;span style="font-weight:bold"&gt;(A) MOE’s Medical Appeal Exercise
(MAE): Appeal to Transfer School for Serious Medical Condition or Severe
Physical Impairments only&lt;/span&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>If you have been posted to Kent Ridge Secondary School, but assess that
the school is unable to accommodate your child’s medical or physical health
needs, you may appeal to MOE for a transfer out. Appeals can be filed at
the school’s general office in person or via email by 12:00pm on Thursday,
21 December 2023.</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>All appeals must be accompanied by relevant supporting documents, such
as medical reports, letters from certified doctors, assessment reports
from qualified medical professionals, etc.</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>The appeal results will be released on 2 January 2024.</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;span style="font-weight:bold"&gt;(B) Students who are posted to Kent
Ridge Secondary School but would like to seek a transfer OUT due to other
(non-medical) reasons&lt;/span&gt;</p>
<p>&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>• Refer to your &lt;u&gt;preferred school’s&lt;/u&gt; website for more
information.&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>• You should still report to KRSS on 2 January 2024 if you do not hear
from your preferred school by then&lt;br&gt;</p>
<p>• While awaiting reply from the other school, you will not need to purchase
KRSS uniforms and books&lt;br&gt;</p>
